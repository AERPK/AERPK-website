Who We Are

The Association for Ecological Restoration Practitioners in Kenya (AERPK) is a professional organization dedicated to advancing the science and practice of ecological restoration. Our members include researchers, practitioners, policymakers, and community leaders committed to restoring Kenya's diverse ecosystems.

Our Mission

Our mission is to promote ecological restoration through research, education, collaboration, and practical implementation. We aim to support the recovery of degraded ecosystems, enhance biodiversity, and ensure sustainable livelihoods for local communities.

Our Vision

We envision a Kenya where restored ecosystems thrive, biodiversity flourishes, and communities benefit from the sustainable use of natural resources.

What We Do

  • Research and Innovation: AERPK supports and conducts cutting-edge research in restoration ecology. We collaborate with universities, research institutions, and international organizations to develop and disseminate innovative restoration techniques and strategies.
  • Education and Outreach: We offer training programs, workshops, and seminars to build the capacity of restoration practitioners. Our educational initiatives aim to raise awareness about the importance of ecological restoration among stakeholders, including local communities, policymakers, and the private sector.
  • Policy Advocacy: AERPK engages with government agencies and other stakeholders to advocate for policies that support ecological restoration. We provide expert advice and technical assistance to ensure that restoration efforts are integrated into national and regional development plans.
  • Community Engagement: We work closely with local communities to implement restoration projects that are culturally appropriate and economically viable. Our community-based approach ensures that restoration efforts are sustainable and have lasting benefits for people and nature.
  • Networking and Collaboration: AERPK provides a platform for networking and collaboration among restoration practitioners. We facilitate the exchange of knowledge and best practices through conferences, symposia, and online forums.

Our Projects

Featured Projects

  • Forest Restoration in the Mau Complex: Restoring indigenous forests and enhancing biodiversity through community-led initiatives.
  • Grassland Rehabilitation in Laikipia: Improving rangeland health and productivity to support wildlife and pastoralist communities.
  • Mangrove Restoration along the Kenyan Coast: Enhancing coastal resilience and protecting marine ecosystems through mangrove reforestation.
